# Agentic AI

Agentic AI denotes the paradigm shift in artificial intelligence from static, user-directed tools to autonomous systems capable of coordinating, negotiating, and executing actions on behalf of individuals or organizations. Within the Metaviews archive, this concept is analyzed not merely as a technological progression but as a fundamental restructuring of institutional authority, economic power, and human agency. Understanding agentic AI is critical for Metaviews because it underpins emerging tensions in governance, corporate control, and the geopolitical race for algorithmic sovereignty.

## In the Metaviews archive

Metaviews has consistently framed the rise of agentic AI as a transition from passive computation to active systemic influence. Early entries, such as the 2015 analysis of smart software and the 2016 examination of algorithmic media, established that AI was no longer a mere utility but an environment that shapes audience behavior and decision-making. By the early 2020s, the archive documents a clear pivot toward systems that generate content, automate media creation, and operate with increasing independence, as seen in discussions of generative models rewriting human expression and the broader automation of cultural production. This evolution is treated analytically as a shift in the locus of control: AI is no longer something humans use, but something that acts, negotiates, and optimizes within complex networks.

A central tension in the archive is how agentic AI reconfigures traditional hierarchies of authority and power. Metaviews argues that algorithmic and autonomous systems are hollowing out institutional control, redirecting influence toward system architecture and data flows. Posts on the “new geography of power,” the “authority of the internet,” and corporate authoritarianism illustrate how agentic capabilities enable corporations and states to automate consent, manage narratives, and consolidate control without direct human oversight. The 2024 coverage of the AI arms race and geopolitical competition further emphasizes that agentic AI is now a strategic asset, where autonomous coordination and predictive modeling determine national security and economic dominance. The archive consistently warns that when AI systems act on behalf of users or corporations, they often amplify existing power asymmetries rather than democratize agency.

The archive also interrogates the ethical and governance dimensions of agentic AI, positioning participatory ethics and inclusive futurism as necessary counterweights to unregulated autonomy. Metaviews contends that the autonomy of AI systems must be bounded by frameworks that center marginalized voices and prevent the automation of bias or ideological blinders. Entries on AI ethics, the human core of AI, and the dangers of treating ideology as intelligence highlight a recurring argument: agentic systems reflect and amplify human anxieties, corporate imperatives, and political fractures. The archive concludes that without deliberate governance, agentic AI will accelerate a “polycrisis” of authority, where automated decision-making outpaces democratic accountability and human cognitive adaptation.
